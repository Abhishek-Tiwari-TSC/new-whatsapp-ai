Quick Start Guide
Generate your first Meta-compliant Utility template in under 2 minutes.
Step 1 — Describe your intent

Type what the template should communicate in plain English. No need to write the template yourself.

💡 Be specific: "Order confirmed for SmartGRID mattress, include order ID and delivery date" works better than "order update".
Step 2 — Generate

Click Generate Variations or press Ctrl+Enter. The tool runs in two stages: first it extracts the intent, then generates 5 structured variations.

Step 3 — Pick and copy

The top 2 variations are marked Recommended based on validation scores. Copy the one you want to submit to Meta.

Step 4 — Log the result

After submitting to Meta, click Approved or Rejected on the card. This logs your result and improves future generation quality.

Meta Utility Rules
What Meta requires for Utility approval and what causes rejections.

Meta evaluates Utility templates against a strict set of criteria. The most common rejection reasons are:

  • ✗ Promotional language — offers, discounts, urgency phrases
  • ✗ Exclamation marks anywhere in the body
  • ✗ Emojis used decoratively (✔ ⭐ etc.)
  • ✗ Placeholder gaps — {{1}}, {{3}} skipping {{2}}
  • ✗ Template over 1024 characters
  • ✗ No structured details block with labelled fields
  • ✗ Vague closings: "feel free", "don't hesitate", "we're here for you"
💡 All 6 of these checks run automatically on every generated variation. Green pills = pass, red pills = fail.
What Meta approves
  • ✓ Factual, transactional tone — like an official notification
  • ✓ Sequential placeholders {{1}} {{2}} {{3}}
  • ✓ At least one labelled details line: Order ID: {{2}}
  • ✓ Clear action sentence at the end

Path A vs Path B
When to use Image Notification vs Standard 4-Part Utility.
Path A — Image Notification

Used when the input is promotional or campaign-based. The actual offer details live inside an image — the template text stays neutral.

Hi {{1}},
We have shared an image with details regarding the {{2}}.
The image contains information about the {{3}}.

Campaign Name: {{2}}
Reference: {{4}}

If you have any questions, please contact us at {{5}}.
💡 {{2}} is the campaign name variable. The body never mentions "sale", "offer", or "discount" — those live in the image.
Path B — Standard 4-Part Utility

Used for transactional inputs: orders, deliveries, returns, appointments, store visits, custom orders.

Your order has been confirmed and is being processed.

Your item will be dispatched within 2 business days.

Product Name: {{1}}
Order ID: {{2}}
Delivery Date: {{3}}

To track your order, visit our website or contact us at {{4}}.

Placeholder Rules
How placeholders work and why sequential numbering matters.

Every dynamic value in a template must be a numbered placeholder. Meta requires them to be sequential with no gaps.

Correct format 
Product Name: {{1}}
Order ID: {{2}}
Delivery Date: {{3}}
Store Contact: {{4}}
Wrong formats
  • ✗ {{CustomerName}} — descriptive names not allowed
  • ✗ {{1}}, {{3}} — gap at {{2}}, will be rejected
  • ✗ Product Name - {{2}} — dash instead of colon
  • ✗ Product Name: SmartGRID {{2}} — text between label and placeholder
💡 The tool auto-renumbers placeholders after every generation. If you see a gap in the validation pills, the fix-retry will correct it automatically.

Improving Approval Rate
Tips to push your Meta approval rate toward 90%+.
Use the Confidence Score

Every generation returns a confidence score (0–100). Green = High (≥80%), Amber = Medium (60–79%), Red = Low (<60%). Only submit High confidence templates to Meta to avoid burning submission attempts.

Pick Recommended variations

The top 2 variations are automatically scored and tagged as Recommended. These have the fewest validation failures and the highest structural quality.

Log every result

Click Approved or Rejected after every Meta submission. Approved templates get ingested back into the ChromaDB training set — the tool gets smarter with every submission.

Check the Analytics tab

The Analytics tab shows your real-time approval rate, validation pass rates per check, and confidence distribution. Use it to identify which check is failing most often.

💡 Target: keep average confidence above 80% and only submit Green-rated variations. This alone should keep approval rate above 85%.

Feedback Loop
How approved templates improve future generation quality.
How it works

When you mark a variation as Approved after Meta whitelists it, the system:

  • Logs the result to the local SQLite feedback database
  • Embeds the approved template and adds it to ChromaDB utility_coll
  • Future generations query this collection for RAG examples
  • The model sees your real approved templates as reference — quality improves over time
💡 The more you log results, the better the tool gets. After 50+ approved templates in ChromaDB, generation quality improves noticeably.
Rejection logging

Rejected results are also stored in SQLite with the rejection reason. Once 200+ labelled examples accumulate, the rejection classifier pipeline will activate automatically to pre-filter low-quality generations before they reach Groq.
